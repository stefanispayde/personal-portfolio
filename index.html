PET-FINDER

This is a full MEAN stack utilizing API from Pet-Finder, an organization that has a database of all the adoptable domestic animals in the US, and the shelters that house them. Features include two different ways to search; the first allows the user to choose the zipcode of the area they want to search, type of animal, breed, and gender from a dropdown menu. The second option is where the user can put in a zipcode and view all the shelters affiliated with Pet-Finder, along with their contact informtion. Another feature within the app enables the user to create a wishlist of the animals they are interested in getting more information on to save and view at any time. Software/Languages/Frameworks used: MongoDB, Express.js, Node.js, EJS, HTML, CSS, Sessions, Angular.js, RESTful routing

Just The Tip

For this web app, I wanted an idea that could easily follow the requirements for full CRUD, but also had a real purpose and addressed a topic of importance or interest. Just The Tip is an advice forum where both men and women can anonymously post questions, concerns, and share both successes and ultimate fails about sex. Through tasteful adult humor, the app can address serious issues, yet maintain an approachability that people can trust. A user can read through all posts other users have posted on site. If a user has a question or story they'd like to share, they can anonymously create a new post. They do not need to be signed in to create a post, but they will if they want to edit or delete it. Comments can be submitted to any post, and posts can be deleted or edited by the user. The user can delete comments on their posts as well.
This app was my first full MEEN stack, in which I used EJS instead of Angular. The two models, posts and comments, have complete RESTful routes which correspond with their respective HTTP methods. Here is where I started weaving together all that I had learned regarding the Rest architectural style, writing Mongo queries and interacting with my document database via the Mongoose module. There were many challenges that I faced while building this app, but it helped me finally piece together the front and back end of web development. I kept the style simple, and kept my focus on completing the app's features and ensuring their accuracy. There are a few features I would like to add such as a way to categorize posts once they are sumbitted, either by week or topic. My work around for now is to provide the user with topic "tags" that they copy and paste in the beginning of their post. This way, other users can scan the page and click on the topics that interest them. This would make the whole app more organized and easier to use, thus bettering the user experience. Software/Languages/Frameworks used: Node.js, Express, EJS, Mongoose, MongoDB, HTML, CSS, RESTful routing
