TIC TAC TOE

This was the very first program I ever built completely by myself using jQuery/Javascript. I was so proud to have the pieces come together, by writing functions that handled the componets necessary for the game's functionality. With several projects now under my belt, I know that that much of this code is redundnt, and there are easier ways of writing a few of these functions. However as a fledgling developer, I was so excited just to have them work, and execute all the requirements and goals that were set! This is also why I leave the code as it, without combing through and rewriting the original code. I like to be reminded of where I started to where I am now. Humbling and inspiring. Software used: HTML5, CSS3, jQuery, Javascript

POSSIBLY MAYBE

When I was 12 years old, Bjork's debut album, appropriately titled Debut, came into my life and cemented her as my idol. Her music is my first choice when I'm designing or making art of any kind, because it has the ability to spur my creative side moe than any other artist. I have always wanted to create a piece in honor of this and decided a pop up book filled with bright colors and typographic design rathe than images. I wanted the typography to stand by itself without pictures, to create texture and not be completely ledgeable. Recognizeable lyrics pop out at you here and there, and then disappear again under layers and layers of rich type. The two pieces you see here were printed on thick paperstock making it sturdy enough to bend where I needed it to. It's size with bleed is 14.375" x 5.0", and makes a perfect tri-fold mini book. I went about printing out several copies of each side, cutting out random pieces and then attaching them to the folded book, in their exact place, making the effect 3D. I donated this piece by request to MarketVision, where I was a graphic Designer, to utilize in showcasing to their clients the different types of work we liked to create. Software used: Adobe Creative Suite, mainly InDesign and Illustrator

SALON AMERICANA

Salon Americana is an older family owned salon that underwent a massive remodel, giving it a new look that was freah and modern. I was on the design team that took over their rebranding, and presented them not only with a new logo, but a new vibe completely. I started with a simple illustration which depicted a womans profile, hair windswept. This was the jumping off point for my logo design in which I took a simple yet clean sans-serif font, broke it apart, and tweaked it until it fit. This was one of the first paid freelance jobs I ever had was a stepping stone in learning how to work with clients, and produce quality work on a deadline.
